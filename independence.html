Pakistan's Independence Day

The Significance of 14th August

Pakistan's Independence Day, celebrated on 14th August, marks the day when Pakistan became an independent nation in 1947. This day is significant as it commemorates the end of British rule in the Indian subcontinent and the establishment of a sovereign nation for Muslims of South Asia.

The independence was the result of a long and arduous struggle by the Muslims of India, under the leadership of Muhammad Ali Jinnah, who is also known as the Father of the Nation. The day is celebrated with great enthusiasm across the country, with flag-hoisting ceremonies, parades, and cultural events.

Key Events Leading to Independence

  • 1906 - Formation of All India Muslim League
  • 1940 - Lahore Resolution demanding a separate nation for Muslims
  • 1947 - Partition of India and creation of Pakistan
